## I. THE FOUNDATIONAL ERA

### John Hopfield (b. July 15, 1933, Chicago, Illinois, USA)
- **Education:** B.S. Physics, Swarthmore College (1954); Ph.D. Physics, Cornell University (1958)
- **Career:** AT&T Bell Labs (solid-state physics); Professor of Physics, Princeton (1964-1980); Professor of Chemistry & Biology, Caltech (1980-1997); returned to Princeton (1997-emeritus)
- **Key Publication:** Invented the **Hopfield network** (1982) -- an associative memory model using concepts from statistical physics (spin glass models). This revived interest in neural networks and helped end the first AI winter.
- **Modern Hopfield Networks:** With Dimitry Krotov (2016), addressed the limited memory of original Hopfield networks
- **Awards:** MacArthur Award (1983-88), Dirac Medal (2001), Albert Einstein World Award of Science (2005), Boltzmann Medal (2022), **Nobel Prize in Physics 2024** (shared with Geoffrey Hinton) "for foundational discoveries and inventions that enable machine learning with artificial neural networks"
- **On AI risk:** At age 91, called himself "very unnerved" by AI advances, compared AI to the discovery of nuclear fission
- **Connection:** His Hopfield network directly inspired Hinton's Boltzmann machine

### Geoffrey Hinton (b. December 6, 1947, London, England) -- "Godfather of AI"
- **Family:** Father Howard Everest Hinton was an entomologist; great-great-grandfather George Boole (Boolean logic); great-grandfather Charles Hinton coined "tesseract"
- **Education:** B.A. Experimental Psychology, Cambridge (1970) -- switched from physiology, philosophy, and physics. Briefly became a carpenter before returning to academia (1972). Ph.D. in AI, University of Edinburgh (1978)
- **Career Trajectory:**
  - Visiting scholar, UC San Diego (1978-80, spring 1982)
  - Faculty, Carnegie Mellon University (1982-87)
  - **University of Toronto** (1987-present emeritus) -- moved to Canada due to disdain for U.S. military funding of AI under Reagan
  - Founded Gatsby Computational Neuroscience Unit, UCL (1998-2001), then returned to Toronto
  - Co-founded **Vector Institute** (2017), chief scientific advisor
  - **Google Brain** (2013-2023), split time with U of T
  - **Resigned from Google** (May 2023) to speak freely about AI risks
- **Key Publications:**
  - **Backpropagation paper** (1986, Nature) with David Rumelhart & Ronald Williams -- "Learning representations by back-propagating errors"
  - **Boltzmann machines** (1983-85) -- using statistical physics tools
  - **AlexNet** (2012, NeurIPS) with Alex Krizhevsky & Ilya Sutskever -- 8-layer CNN that won ImageNet, launching the deep learning revolution
  - Deep belief networks, capsule networks, dropout regularization
- **Awards:** AAAI Fellow (1991), Royal Society of Canada Fellow (1996), Royal Society Fellow (1998), first Rumelhart Prize (2001), Herzberg Gold Medal (2010), **Turing Award 2018** (with Bengio & LeCun), **Nobel Prize in Physics 2024** (with Hopfield), Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering (2025)
- **Nobel speech (Dec 10, 2024, Stockholm):** Warned of AI creating "terrible new viruses and horrendous lethal weapons," called for urgent regulation, criticized tech companies chasing "short-term profits" over safety. Estimated 50% probability AI surpasses human intelligence within 5-20 years.
- **2025 positions:** Urged "nurturing instincts" in AI, proposed a "mother AI" concept to prevent takeover, warned massive unemployment will result from AI
- **Key quote:** "The alarm bell I'm ringing has to do with the existential threat of them taking control."
- **Notable students:** Ilya Sutskever, Alex Krizhevsky, Yann LeCun (postdoc), Peter Dayan, Sam Roweis, Max Welling, Richard Zemel, Brendan Frey, Radford Neal, Yee Whye Teh, Ruslan Salakhutdinov, Alex Graves, Zoubin Ghahramani, George Dahl, Abdel-rahman Mohamed, Jimmy Ba

### Yann LeCun (b. July 8, 1960, Soisy-sous-Montmorency, France)
- **Education:** Diplome d'Ingenieur, ESIEE Paris (1983); Ph.D. Computer Science, Universite Pierre et Marie Curie/Sorbonne (1987) -- thesis on connectionist learning, proposed early backpropagation
- **Career:**
  - **Postdoc with Geoffrey Hinton**, University of Toronto (1987-88)
  - **AT&T Bell Labs** (1988-2003) -- developed **Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs/LeNet)**, "Optimal Brain Damage," Graph Transformer Networks. Bank check recognition system read >10% of all U.S. checks
  - **NYU** (2003-present) -- Jacob T. Schwartz Professor, Courant Institute. Founded NYU Center for Data Science (2012)
  - **Meta/Facebook AI Research (FAIR)** founding director (Dec 2013), then **Chief AI Scientist** (2013-2025)
  - **Left Meta** (end of 2025) -- founded **AMI Labs** (Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs), HQ in Paris, focused on "world models" (JEPA architecture). Raised $1.03 billion at $3.5 billion valuation (March 2026)
- **Awards:** IEEE Neural Network Pioneer Award (2014), **Turing Award 2018** (with Hinton & Bengio), Princess of Asturias Award (2022), Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering (2025), member of US National Academy of Sciences & Engineering, French Academie des Sciences
- **AI Risk Position:** Considers existential risk discussion "premature" and "preposterous." Believes superintelligent machines will have no desire for self-preservation. Opposed California's SB 1047 AI safety bill (which Hinton endorsed). Argues LLMs are a "dead end" for true intelligence.
- **Key quote on disagreement:** Posted photo with Hinton and Bengio: "A reminder that people can disagree about important things but still be good friends."
- **Connection to Hinton:** Postdoc under Hinton; they are two of the three "Godfathers of Deep Learning"

### Yoshua Bengio (b. March 5, 1964, Paris, France)
- **Origin:** Born to Moroccan Jewish parents who embraced 1960s counterculture; family moved to Montreal when he was 12
- **Education:** B.Eng. Electrical Engineering (1986), M.Sc. Computer Science (1988), Ph.D. Computer Science (1991) -- all from McGill University
- **Postdocs:** MIT (with Michael I. Jordan), Bell Labs (with Yann LeCun -- worked on handwriting recognition)
- **Career:**
  - Faculty, **Universite de Montreal** (1993-present) -- full professor since 2002
  - Founded **MILA** (Montreal Institute for Learning Algorithms, now Quebec AI Institute) -- scientific director until 2025
  - CIFAR AI Chair
- **Key Contributions:**
  - Neural probabilistic language model (word embeddings) -- overcoming "curse of dimensionality" in NLP
  - Contributed to GANs, soft-attention mechanisms (precursor to Transformer attention)
  - Deep learning theoretical foundations in 2000s
- **Awards:** **Turing Award 2018** (with Hinton & LeCun), Killam Prize, Herzberg Gold Medal, Princess of Asturias Award, VinFuture Prize, Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering (2025)
- **Citations:** Most-cited computer scientist globally; first AI researcher with >1 million Google Scholar citations (November 2025)
- **AI Safety:** Strong advocate. Chairs the **International AI Safety Report**. Contributed to the Montreal Declaration for Responsible AI. TIME 100 most influential (2024).
- **2025:** Launched nonprofit **LawZero** (June 2025) to build "honest" AI systems that detect/block harmful autonomous agent behavior
- **Connection:** Supervised Ian Goodfellow's PhD; postdoc with LeCun at Bell Labs; third "Godfather of Deep Learning"

## V. KEY THEMATIC THREADS

### The "Toronto School" / Hinton's Lab
Geoffrey Hinton's University of Toronto lab is the single most influential node in the AI pioneer network. Notable graduates/postdocs include:
- **Yann LeCun** (postdoc 1987-88) --> CNNs, Meta FAIR, AMI Labs
- **Ilya Sutskever** (PhD) --> AlexNet, OpenAI, SSI
- **Alex Krizhevsky** (PhD) --> AlexNet
- **Peter Dayan** --> Gatsby Unit, Max Planck
- **Radford Neal** --> Bayesian neural networks
- **Brendan Frey** --> Deep Genomics founder
- **Ruslan Salakhutdinov** --> CMU professor, Apple AI director
- **Zoubin Ghahramani** --> Cambridge, Uber AI Labs
- **Max Welling** --> University of Amsterdam
- **Jimmy Ba** --> University of Toronto professor, joined Musk's xAI
- **George Dahl, Abdel-rahman Mohamed** --> deep learning for speech recognition

The lab was sustained through AI winters by **CIFAR** (Canadian Institute for Advanced Research) funding when U.S. and U.K. funding dried up. Canada's role as an AI haven (Hinton moved there in 1987 to avoid U.S. military funding) created the conditions for the Toronto-Montreal axis that produced much of modern AI.

### The "Three Godfathers of Deep Learning" / Turing Award 2018
**Hinton, Bengio, and LeCun** shared the 2018 ACM Turing Award "for conceptual and engineering breakthroughs that have made deep neural networks a critical component of computing." They persevered through AI winters when neural networks were dismissed:
- Hinton: Boltzmann machines (1985), backpropagation (1986), deep belief nets (2006), AlexNet (2012)
- LeCun: CNNs/LeNet (1989), practical backpropagation demonstrations
- Bengio: Word embeddings, attention mechanisms, deep learning theory

### The AI Winters
**First AI Winter (~1974-1980):** Triggered by Minsky & Papert's *Perceptrons* (1969) exposing neural network limitations; Lighthill Report (1973, UK) cut funding. Hopfield, Werbos, Fukushima kept working.

**Second AI Winter (~1987-2000):** Overpromising on expert systems crashed expectations. Neural networks considered a "dead end." Researchers rebranded work as "informatics," "knowledge-based systems," "computational intelligence." Hinton, LeCun, Bengio persisted -- funded by CIFAR, Bell Labs, and sheer conviction.

**Revival:** Hopfield network (1982) --> Boltzmann machines (1985) --> Backpropagation (1986) --> LeNet (1989) --> Deep belief nets (2006) --> AlexNet (2012) --> modern era.

### The Hinton-LeCun Disagreement on AI Risk
The most prominent public split among the "Godfathers":
- **Hinton + Bengio:** AI poses existential risk; support regulation (SB 1047); Hinton gives 50% chance AI surpasses humans in 5-20 years
- **LeCun (+ Andrew Ng):** Existential risk is "premature" and "preposterous"; LLMs are a dead end; regulation would harm open research
- Despite intensity, they remain friends: "People can disagree about important things but still be good friends" --LeCun

### The OpenAI Exodus
Multiple waves of departures:

**2020-2021 (Anthropic founding):** Dario Amodei (VP Research), Daniela Amodei, and 14 researchers left over safety/direction disagreements --> founded Anthropic

**2024 Major departures:**
- Ilya Sutskever (May 2024) --> SSI
- Jan Leike (May 2024, Superalignment co-lead) --> Anthropic
- Mira Murati (CTO, Sept 2024) --> Thinking Machines Lab (Feb 2025)
- Bob McGrew (CRO), Barret Zoph (VP Research) -- Sept 2024
- Alec Radford (Dec 2024) --> independent research
- Miles Brundage (Oct 2024)
- Daniel Kokotajlo revealed ~half of AGI safety team gone

**2025 (Meta Superintelligence Lab wave):** 7+ researchers to Meta's new Superintelligence Lab including Shengjia Zhao (co-creator of ChatGPT), Jiahui Yu, Hongyu Ren. Also: Liam Fedus (VP Research) --> Periodic Labs; Larry Summers resigned from board; John Schulman --> Thinking Machines Lab.

By 2025, Sam Altman was one of only two remaining active original founding team members.

### 2024 Nobel Prizes in AI
An unprecedented year: AI work recognized across two Nobel categories:
- **Physics:** John Hopfield & Geoffrey Hinton -- "foundational discoveries and inventions that enable machine learning with artificial neural networks"
- **Chemistry:** Demis Hassabis & John Jumper (half) + David Baker (half) -- protein structure prediction (AlphaFold) and computational protein design
